# Master Project Map — Adaptive Coherence Research Program

## 1) Core Thesis
This project proposes that ADHD, anhedonia, treatment-resistant depression, brain fog, and parts of early neurodegenerative decline may share an upstream architecture of prefrontal energetic instability rather than being fully separate disorders. The central claim is that the prefrontal cortex functions as a vulnerability bottleneck because it is unusually energy-hungry, metabolically fragile, and highly dependent on signal fidelity, buffering reserve, inhibitory stability, methylation support, and repair-permissive terrain.

## 2) Deepest Framework
The broadest conceptual frame is adaptive coherence under constraint: living systems must preserve organized function while dealing with limited energy, variable stress, noise, and perturbation. The older Energy Resistance Principle supplies the friction-and-flow layer. The newer framework extends that model by adding buffering reserve, volatility over time, network specificity, and terrain permissiveness. In plain language: health is not just about having fuel. It is about being able to route, buffer, stabilize, and use that fuel under real demand.

## 3) Main Biological Modules
### A. Energy / phosphocreatine buffering
- Phosphocreatine (PCr) reserve and creatine kinase buffering
- Dynamic response to demand spikes, not just resting fuel availability
- Prefrontal vulnerability under cognitive load

### B. Methylation / one-carbon metabolism
- SAMe availability
- GAA-to-creatine methylation cost
- Homocysteine management via TMG, folate, B12, B6
- Glycine balance and GNMT/AGAT relevance

### C. Inhibitory stability / signal-to-noise
- Tonic inhibition via extrasynaptic delta-containing GABA-A receptors
- ADHD as partly a noise-control problem, not only a dopamine problem
- GAA + BHB as a possible convergent modulation route

### D. Epigenetic flexibility / repair gating
- G9a / EHMT2 silencing logic
- TET / demethylation / plasticity restoration logic
- BHB, vitamin C, vitamin A, sulforaphane, and related repair-support concepts

### E. Oxygenation / vascular / gasotransmitter support
- Prefrontal oxygenation
- NO / H2S / CO biology
- Mitochondrial and vascular support as part of the same system

### F. Ketogenic adaptation / emergency brain energetics
- BHB as both fuel and signaling molecule
- Possible brain-autonomous ketone circuitry
- Tiered stress-energy model from ordinary glucose use to local emergency ketone support

## 4) Leading Intervention Architecture
The current lead intervention model is the PEC / ignition-stack concept:
- GAA
- creatine monohydrate
- betaine / TMG
- ketogenic background

Then layered additions:
- sulforaphane
- NACET
- fatty fish / LPC omega-3 support
- dietary nitrate / leafy greens
- cofactors relevant to methylation and gasotransmitter support

The stack is not framed as a random supplement list. It is framed as a systems intervention aimed at restoring a damaged supply chain.

## 5) Main Phenomenological Insight
One of the project’s strongest original ideas is that recovery may occur in sequence rather than all at once:
- cost of action can fall before reward returns
- function can improve before pleasure fully returns
- behavioral initiation can recover before emotional color or hedonic richness normalize

This is treated as expected biology, not failure.

## 7) Current Evidence Tiers
### Established / relatively strong
- PFC is unusually metabolically expensive and vulnerable
- phosphocreatine buffering matters for rapid ATP deployment
- creatine faces CNS transport limitations
- ketosis can alter fuel use and signaling in the brain
- subjective symptoms can diverge sharply from standard normal lab impressions

### Emerging but promising
- GAA as a superior brain-delivery creatine precursor
- GAA’s relevance to tonic inhibition and ADHD-like signal/noise problems
- prefrontal oxygenation benefits of creatine+GAA
- adaptive coherence framework linking flow, buffering, volatility, and phenomenology

### Proposed synthesis / hypothesis-generating
- unified model spanning ADHD, anhedonia, depression, dementia, and brain fog
- recovery sequencing where reward lags function because different circuits come back online on different clocks
- combined multi-layer protocol as a coherent prefrontal restoration strategy

## 8) Current Weak Points / Credibility Risks
- several synthesis moves outrun direct human clinical confirmation
- GAA human literature is concentrated in a relatively small research base
- the n=1 case is compelling but still a single case with simultaneous interventions
- some mechanistic bridges are strong in theory but not yet directly validated as a full combined protocol
- the project must carefully separate established findings, emerging evidence, and proposed synthesis
