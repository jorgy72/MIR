# Master Outline — Adaptive Coherence Under Constraint

## Working Title
**Adaptive Coherence Under Constraint: A Unified Neurometabolic Framework for Prefrontal Failure and Repair**

## Purpose of This Document
This outline is the canonical structure for Version 1 of the main paper. Its job is to turn the project from a scattered theory-and-notes archive into a coherent research document with clear evidence tiers, a restrained tone, and a strong translational center.

---

## 1. Executive Summary
### Goal
State the central claim in one page.

### Core points to include
- ADHD, anhedonia, treatment-resistant depression, brain fog, and parts of early neurodegenerative decline may share upstream biological bottlenecks rather than being fully separate disorders.
- The prefrontal cortex is the central anatomical vulnerability hub.
- The deepest frame is not simple energy deficiency, but impaired adaptive coherence under constraint.
- Recovery depends on flow, buffering, inhibitory stability, methylation support, and repair-permissive terrain.
- The paper is integrative and hypothesis-generating, not a claim of final proof.

---

## 2. The Problem With Existing Framing
### Goal
Explain why current symptom-silo models underperform.

### Core points to include
- Modern clinical categories often separate conditions that may share upstream biology.
- Symptom management can improve behavior without repairing substrate-level failure.
- Standard bloodwork may miss regional and dynamic energetic instability.
- Patients are often misread as unmotivated or psychologically failing when the problem may be impaired energetic deployment.

## 4. Why the Prefrontal Cortex Is the Bottleneck
### Goal
Anchor the whole framework anatomically.

### Core points to include
- The PFC is unusually energy-intensive and metabolically fragile
- It is highly dependent on millisecond-scale ATP support, inhibitory tone, and signal fidelity
- It governs executive function, sustained attention, working memory, impulse control, emotional regulation, and reward-directed behavior
- High-order cognition fails early when buffering and terrain degrade

## 12. Predictions and Falsifiability
### Goal
Make the framework act like a research program rather than a worldview.

### Core predictions
- Functional recovery often precedes hedonic recovery
- Reserve-sensitive perturbations reveal deficits better than resting metrics alone
- Some interventions become more effective only after terrain improves
- Recovery will be regionally uneven rather than globally uniform
- qEEG coherence, HRV, sleep quality, and initiation cost should move together more coherently than symptom labels alone predict
- Biomarker and imaging work should increasingly focus on dynamic reserve rather than static abundance
